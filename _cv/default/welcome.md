    <h3>Short Bio</h3>
     <div style="text-align: left;">
           <p>
       I studied biology at the Philipps-Universität Marburg with major in Microbiology and did my diploma thesis (equivalent to MSc) at the Max Planck Institute for Terrestrial Microbiology in Marburg. I did my graduate training in the International Leibniz Research School for Microbial and Biomolecular Interactions (ILRS) and Jena School for Microbial Communication (JSMC) and became Doctoral and Postdoctoral Researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Chemical Ecology in Jena. For my PhD I did six research trips to Utah (USA) to do field work on plant-microbe-herbivore interactions. As postdoctoral researchert at Freie Universität Berlin I shifted my focus on insect-microbe symbiosisas and started working with butterflies at the Collaborative Research Center (CRC 973). Now I am a researcher at Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität München (LMU Munich) at the Center for Organismic Adaptation (CORA) working on pollinator-microbe interactions.
